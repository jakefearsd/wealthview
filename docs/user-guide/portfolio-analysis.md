# Portfolio Analysis

WealthView provides a dashboard and per-account views to help you understand your financial position at a glance. This guide explains what each visualization shows and how to interpret it.

---

## Dashboard Overview

When you log in, the **Dashboard** is the first thing you see. It presents a high-level summary of your entire financial picture across all accounts and properties.

---

## Net Worth Card

At the top of the dashboard, the **Net Worth** card displays a single number representing your total net worth. This is calculated as:

**Net Worth = Investment Value + Property Equity + Cash**

Where:

- **Investment Value** — The sum of market values across all holdings in all investment accounts (brokerage, IRA, 401k, Roth).
- **Property Equity** — The current value of each property minus its outstanding mortgage balance.
- **Cash** — Bank account balances.

This number updates whenever prices change, property values are refreshed, or you record new transactions.

---

## Asset Allocation Chart

A **pie chart** breaks down your net worth by category, showing how your wealth is distributed across:

- Different account types (brokerage, IRA, 401k, Roth, bank).
- Property equity.

This view helps you see whether your assets are concentrated in one area or diversified across account types. For example, you might notice that 60% of your net worth is in a single 401(k), suggesting limited tax diversification.

---

## Account Balance Cards

Below the summary cards, each account appears as an individual card showing:

- **Account name** and institution.
- **Current market value** — The total value of all holdings in the account.
- **Account type** indicator (brokerage, IRA, etc.).

Click on any account card to navigate to its detail page for deeper analysis.

---

## Combined Portfolio History Chart

The dashboard includes a **portfolio history chart** that aggregates the value of all your investment accounts over time. This line chart shows how your total portfolio value has trended.

You can apply a **years filter** to focus on a specific time window — for example, viewing just the last year of performance or the full history going back to the earliest available price data.

The combined chart sums the individual account values at each date, giving you a single view of your overall investment trajectory.

---

## Per-Account Analysis

Clicking on an account from the dashboard or the Accounts page takes you to the **account detail page**, which provides account-level analysis.

### Portfolio History Chart

Each account has its own portfolio history chart showing the value of that specific account over time. This uses the account's current holdings multiplied by daily historical prices.

Use this chart to understand how a single account has performed independently of your other accounts.

### Holdings Breakdown

A table lists every security held in the account with:

| Column | What It Shows |
|--------|--------------|
| **Symbol** | The ticker symbol. |
| **Quantity** | Shares held. |
| **Cost Basis** | Your total cost for the position. |
| **Market Value** | Current value (quantity times latest price). |
| **Gain/Loss ($)** | Dollar difference between market value and cost basis. |
| **Gain/Loss (%)** | Percentage return on the position. |

This table helps you identify which positions are performing well and which are underperforming.

### Interpreting Gain/Loss

- A **positive gain** means the current value exceeds what you paid. The position is "in the money."
- A **negative gain** (a loss) means the current value is below your cost basis. This is an unrealized loss — it only becomes realized when you sell.
- Gain/loss percentages let you compare returns across positions of different sizes. A $500 gain on a $1,000 position (50%) is a stronger return than a $500 gain on a $10,000 position (5%).

---

## Tips for Analysis

- **Review your dashboard regularly** to stay aware of your overall financial position and catch any unexpected changes.
- **Compare account-level charts** to see which accounts are driving your overall portfolio growth.
- **Watch the asset allocation chart** for concentration risk. If one account type dominates, consider whether rebalancing makes sense for your situation.
- **Use the portfolio history chart time filters** to distinguish between long-term trends and short-term volatility.
